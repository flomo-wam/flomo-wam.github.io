Abstract

Motion mediates the transfer from video to action

Pretrained video models carry internet-scale motion priors, but predicting full video is wasteful for action. We argue dense 3D motion is how that prior best transfers to action. FloMo is a 5B video model fine-tuned to predict 3D scene flow and actions jointly: scene flow is rendered as RGB and encoded into the video latent space, so no new tokenizer is needed. Co-trained on action-free human video and robot teleoperation, it generalizes to unseen objects, motion primitives, and tasks.

Removing the motion objective hurts generalization; adding a video objective hurts success. Action tokens attend roughly 3× more to motion than to video — motion mediates the transfer from video to action.

Contributions

What we show

01

Motion as the WAM prediction target

We render 3D scene flow as RGB and encode it with a frozen video VAE, so motion shares the video latent space. One backbone flow-matches motion and action — no new tokenizer.

02

Motion is the most effective target

On real-robot manipulation, predicting motion beats predicting video — or both — on in-distribution success and OOD generalization. Adding a video target interferes with action learning.

03

Generalization from human video

FloMo transfers to objects, motions, and tasks seen only in action-free human video — like pulling a string, never present in robot training.

Method

3D scene flow as the video-action intermediate

The action that creates a future state depends on how the scene moves, not how it looks. 2D optical flow mixes camera motion with task motion, so FloMo predicts 3D scene flow: the 3D displacement of each tracked point in the first camera frame, isolating object and hand motion.

We render this as an RGB video and encode it with the frozen Wan 2.2 VAE. Rendered flow lands in the same latent space as video, so the pretrained DiT handles it with no new tokenizer — we fine-tune only the backbone (LoRA, rank 64).

Two-stage co-training

FloMo is co-trained on a mixture of egocentric human video and bimanual robot teleoperation.

Stage 1
Mid-training

Train on the full mixture. Human video supervises scene flow only; robot data supervises scene flow and action — building a broad motion prior at scale.

→
Stage 2
Fine-tuning

Train on robot data only, dropping human video. This sharpens action prediction without diluting the gradient with action-free samples.

At inference, from an image and a language instruction, FloMo denoises for 10 UniPC steps, then executes a chunk of 16 actions before re-planning.

Generalization from human video

Scene flow transfers from human video
